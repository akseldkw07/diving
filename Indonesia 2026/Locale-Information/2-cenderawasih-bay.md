# Cenderawasih Bay

## Overview
Indonesia's largest marine national park, and one of the only places on earth where you can reliably dive with whale sharks. The whale sharks here are attracted to bagan fishing platforms (traditional lift-net boats), creating unique encounters where massive whale sharks feed alongside fishermen. Located in West Papua, this is remote and wild.

## Dates
- **Window:** June preferred — peak liveaboard operations run June–October. May is shoulder (fewer boats running).
- **Season:** May = shoulder (liveaboard availability limited). June = beginning of peak season.
- **Weather:** Dry season begins. Calm conditions from June onward.

## Diving

### Conditions
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Visibility | 10–30m (variable) |
| Water temp | 27°C (81°F) May–Oct |
| Currents | Calm during main season (Jun–Sep) |
| Wetsuit | 3mm |

### Seasonal Events (May–June)
- Whale sharks present year-round — attracted to bagan fishing boats
- June onward is more reliable for liveaboard operations
- 150+ coral species, 200+ fish species
- WWII wrecks (Japanese harbor facilities) scattered through the bay

### Key Dive Sites
| Site | Depth | Highlights | Difficulty |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| Kwatisore Bay (bagan platforms) | 5–20m | Whale shark encounters while they feed at fishing nets | Easy–Moderate |
| WWII wrecks | 10–30m | Japanese harbor infrastructure, coral-encrusted | Moderate |
| Reef sites | 5–25m | Pristine hard and soft coral, reef fish | Easy–Moderate |

### Dive Plan
- 5–7 days as part of a liveaboard route
- Whale shark encounters are the main event — multiple sessions over several days
- Mix in reef dives and WWII wreck exploration
- Nitrox useful but not critical (moderate depths)

## Logistics

### Getting There
- Fly to **Manokwari** or **Nabire** (via Jakarta or Sorong)
- Or board a liveaboard from **Sorong** that includes Cenderawasih on its route
- Domestic connections can be unreliable — arrive a day early

### Getting Out
- Fly out via Manokwari/Nabire or continue liveaboard to Sorong/Raja Ampat

### Accommodation
- Liveaboard is the standard (and really only practical) option
- Basic accommodation available in Nabire or Manokwari for pre/post nights

### Costs
| Item | Estimate (USD) |
|------|---------------|
| Liveaboard (5–7 nights, part of route) | $3,000–6,000 |
| Domestic flights to Manokwari/Nabire | $150–300 |
| National park fee | ~$25–50 |

### Practical Info
- **Visa:** Indonesia e-VoA
- **Currency:** IDR. Very limited cash infrastructure — bring everything you need.
- **Connectivity:** Minimal to none.
- **Hyperbaric chamber:** None locally. Nearest in Sorong or Manado — evacuation required.

## Notes
- Swimming with whale sharks at fishing platforms is a unique, unforgettable experience
- Best combined with a Raja Ampat liveaboard that extends to Cenderawasih
- May availability is limited — June onward is when most operations run
- The bay is enormous — 14,500 km² marine park
- Pack for remote: backup batteries, entertainment, seasickness meds
