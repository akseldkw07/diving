# Nusa Penida

## Overview
The manta ray capital of Bali. Cleaning stations on the south coast produce near-daily manta encounters, and May is the absolute peak month. Easy access from Bali makes this a low-friction start or addition to any Indonesia trip. Crystal Bay is also famous for mola mola (ocean sunfish) from July–November.

## Dates
- **Window:** Flexible — easy to slot in as a 3–5 day segment before or after other legs
- **Season:** Peak — May is the best month for mantas. Dry season underway, warm water, calm seas.
- **Weather:** Dry, sunny, occasional afternoon clouds. Excellent surface conditions.

## Diving

### Conditions
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Visibility | 15–20m (north/east sites); ~15m south side |
| Water temp | 26–29°C in May; starts cooling from late June |
| Currents | Rare at manta sites (sheltered bays); surge possible from ocean swell |
| Wetsuit | 3mm full suit |

### Seasonal Events (May–June)
- **Manta rays:** Peak mating activity in May — almost guaranteed daily encounters at cleaning stations
- Mola mola season starts July (outside this window)
- Reef sharks, bamboo sharks, blue-spotted stingrays active
- Healthy hard coral gardens in peak condition

### Key Dive Sites
| Site | Depth | Highlights | Difficulty |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| Manta Point (south coast) | 5–15m | Cleaning station, daily mantas, reef sharks, stingrays | Moderate |
| Crystal Bay | 5–30m | Protected bay, beautiful corals, mola mola (Jul–Nov) | Easy–Moderate |
| SD Point | 5–20m | Hard coral gardens, sweetlips, batfish schools | Easy |
| Gamat Bay | 5–18m | Sheltered bay, macro life, colorful reef | Easy |
| Toyapakeh | 5–30m | Wall dive, soft corals, strong current possible | Moderate |

### Dive Plan
- 3–5 days, 2–3 dives/day = 8–15 dives total
- Hit Manta Point multiple times (conditions vary day to day)
- Mix in Crystal Bay and SD Point for reef variety
- Nitrox not critical here (mostly shallow) but nice to have

## Logistics

### Getting There
- International flight → **Bali (DPS)**
- Fast boat from Sanur to Nusa Penida (~30 min, multiple daily departures)
- Some operators arrange pickup from Bali hotels

### Getting Out
- Ferry back to Bali → domestic flight to next destination
- Or stay in Bali for a buffer day before flying onward

### Accommodation
- Guesthouses and small resorts on Nusa Penida, $30–100/night
- Some dive operators offer accommodation packages
- Stay near Toyapakeh or Crystal Bay for proximity to dive boats

### Costs
| Item | Estimate (USD) |
|------|---------------|
| Diving (10–12 dives) | $400–600 |
| Accommodation (5 nights) | $150–500 |
| Fast boat Bali ↔ Nusa Penida | $30–50 roundtrip |
| Food & transport on island | $15–25/day |

### Practical Info
- **Visa:** Indonesia e-VoA, IDR 500,000 (~$35)
- **Currency:** IDR. ATMs on Nusa Penida (limited). Bring backup cash from Bali.
- **Connectivity:** Decent 4G on most of the island. WiFi at hotels.
- **Hyperbaric chamber:** Sanglah Hospital in Denpasar, Bali (~1.5 hrs by boat + car). BIMC Hospital also in Bali.
- **Roads:** Nusa Penida roads are rough — rent a scooter or arrange transport through your operator.

## Notes
- May = best manta month, period. If mantas are a priority, don't skip this.
- Easy to combine with Bali arrival/departure days
- Much less developed than mainland Bali — embrace the island vibe
- Water can be choppy on the south side — Dramamine if needed
