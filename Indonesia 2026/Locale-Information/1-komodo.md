# Komodo National Park

## Overview
World-class drift diving in a UNESCO World Heritage site. Mantas at cleaning stations, reef sharks on current-swept pinnacles, and some of the most vibrant coral in the Coral Triangle. Strong currents make this a playground for experienced divers.

## Dates
- **Window:** May 21–28 (per itinerary, Leg 2)
- **Season:** Peak — dry season underway, warm water, best visibility of the year
- **Weather:** Minimal rain, calm surface conditions, occasional afternoon clouds

## Diving

### Conditions
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Visibility | 15–30m (excellent) |
| Water temp | 26–29°C (79–84°F) |
| Currents | Strong. Advanced OW minimum for most sites |
| Thermoclines | Possible on south side — can drop to 22°C at depth |
| Wetsuit | 3mm full suit; hood useful for south side thermoclines |

### Seasonal Events (May–June)
- Manta rays at peak activity — cleaning stations and feeding aggregations (Apr–Sep season)
- Reef sharks patrolling current-swept sites
- Eagle rays and mobula rays in channels
- Good macro on calmer south sites (nudis, pygmy seahorses)

### Key Dive Sites
| Site | Depth | Highlights | Difficulty |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| Manta Point (Karang Makassar) | 5–15m | 3km sandbank, manta cleaning stations, shallow drift | Moderate |
| Batu Bolong | 5–40m | Pinnacle, Napoleon wrasse, whitetip sharks, massive fish schools | Advanced |
| Castle Rock | 10–30m | Submerged seamount, grey reef sharks, giant trevally, fusilier walls | Advanced |
| Crystal Rock | 5–25m | Soft corals, reef fish, nearby Castle Rock | Moderate–Advanced |
| The Cauldron / Shotgun | 10–30m | Channel acceleration dive, adrenaline rush | Advanced |
| Tatawa Besar | 5–25m | Gentle drift, coral slope, macro + wide-angle | Moderate |
| Pink Beach | 5–15m | Easy reef dive, post-dive on one of the world's pink sand beaches | Easy |

### Dive Plan
- 7-night liveaboard covering north + south routes
- 3–4 dives/day, ~24–28 dives total
- Nitrox recommended for repetitive deep dives on pinnacles
- Priority: Manta Point (multiple visits), Batu Bolong, Castle Rock

## Logistics

### Getting There
- Fly to **Labuan Bajo (LBJ)** from Bali (DPS) — multiple daily flights, ~1.5 hrs
- Airlines: Wings Air, AirAsia, Batik Air, Garuda
- Arrive evening before liveaboard embarkation

### Getting Out
- Fly Labuan Bajo → Sorong (SOQ) for Raja Ampat leg. No direct flights — connect via Makassar (UPG) or Bali (DPS). Budget a full travel day.
- **Heads up:** May 28 is Eid al-Adha — book flights early, domestic travel surges

### Accommodation
- Pre/post liveaboard: guesthouses in Labuan Bajo ($30–80/night)
- Liveaboard cabin included in trip price

### Practical Info
- **Visa:** Indonesia e-VoA, IDR 500,000 (~$35). Apply at molina.imigrasi.go.id
- **Currency:** IDR. USD accepted by some operators. ATMs in Labuan Bajo.
- **Connectivity:** Good 4G in Labuan Bajo; patchy on liveaboard (satellite if available)
- **Hyperbaric chamber:** Labuan Bajo has a chamber at the local clinic. Bali is the nearest full facility.
