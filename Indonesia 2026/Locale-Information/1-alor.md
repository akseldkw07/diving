# Alor

## Overview
Off-the-beaten-path diving in eastern Indonesia. Alor sits in the strait between Flores and Timor, where cold nutrient-rich upwellings attract an unusual mix of big pelagics — mola mola (ocean sunfish), sperm whales, dolphins, hammerheads, thresher sharks — alongside healthy reefs and excellent macro. Very few divers make it here, and that's part of the appeal.

## Dates
- **Window:** Flexible — dry season runs April–October
- **Season:** Shoulder in May, improving into June. Visibility jumps from ~15m (May plankton blooms) to 40m by mid-June as dry monsoon settles in. Water is very warm (31–32°C).
- **Weather:** Southeast monsoon period. Generally stable, manageable surface conditions.

## Diving

### Conditions
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Visibility | 15m (May, plankton blooms) → 40m (June, dry monsoon) |
| Water temp | 31–32°C (88–90°F) — very warm |
| Currents | Moderate in May–Jun; strongest Oct–Nov. Some sites have strong eddies. |
| Wetsuit | Rashguard or 1mm — you will be warm |
| Experience | Experienced divers recommended due to currents |

### Seasonal Events (May–June)
- Mola mola (ocean sunfish) present — cold upwellings bring them to cleaning stations
- Sperm whale sightings, dolphin pods
- Hammerhead and thresher sharks at current-swept sites
- Eagle rays in channels
- Nutrient-rich plankton blooms support big species (at the cost of vis in early May)

### Key Dive Sites
| Site | Depth | Highlights | Difficulty |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| Great Wall of Pantar | 5–40m | Dramatic wall, soft corals, pelagics in the blue | Advanced |
| Current Alley | 10–30m | Reliable hammerhead encounters, strong current | Advanced |
| Bama Wall | 5–30m | Steep drop-off, colorful resident reef fish | Moderate |
| Kalabahi Bay | 3–20m | Muck diving — frogfish, seahorses, mudskippers | Easy |
| Clown Valley (Apuri) | 5–20m | Anemone fields, clownfish, macro critters | Easy |
| Cathedral | 10–30m | Swim-through formations, soft corals | Moderate |

### Dive Plan
- 4–7 days, land-based or as part of a Komodo → Alor liveaboard extension
- 2–3 dives/day
- Mix of wall dives, current dives, and muck diving in Kalabahi Bay
- Nitrox useful for wall dives and repetitive profiles

## Logistics

### Getting There
- Fly to **Alor (via Kupang)** — small domestic airports
- Or access via liveaboard from Labuan Bajo (Komodo extension)
- Route from Bali: DPS → Kupang (West Timor) → Alor. Limited flight schedules.

### Getting Out
- Fly Alor → Kupang → Bali or onward
- Or liveaboard continues to next destination

### Accommodation
- Small guesthouses and dive lodges — $40–120/night
- Very limited tourism infrastructure — this is not a resort destination
- Dive operators often arrange accommodation packages

### Practical Info
- **Visa:** Indonesia e-VoA
- **Currency:** IDR. ATMs in Kalabahi (capital) — unreliable. Bring cash.
- **Connectivity:** Limited 4G in Kalabahi. Minimal elsewhere.
- **Hyperbaric chamber:** None on Alor. Nearest in Kupang (West Timor) — requires air evacuation. DAN insurance essential.

## Notes
- Alor is for divers who want to go where nobody else does
- Pairs beautifully with Komodo — some liveaboards run combined Komodo + Alor itineraries (10–14 days)
- The mola mola here are more reliable than Nusa Penida for May/June (cold upwellings)
- Very few other divers — you'll likely have sites to yourself
- Infrastructure is basic — manage expectations for accommodation and food
- Best visibility is June onward, so later in the window is better for Alor
