## Seasonal Highlights by Location

### Komodo National Park
**Best Window:** May 21–28 (peak dry season)
**Bad Window:** None in May–June

**Seasonal Events (May 15–Jun 5):**
- 🦈 **Manta rays** at peak activity — cleaning stations & feeding aggregations (Apr–Sep season)
- Reef sharks patrolling current-swept sites
- Eagle rays & mobula rays in channels
- Good macro on calmer south sites (nudis, pygmy seahorses)

**Notes:** May 28 is Eid al-Adha — book flights early. Strong currents require Advanced OW minimum.

---

### Raja Ampat
**Best Window:** May 29–Jun 5 (shoulder season, excellent conditions)
**Bad Window:** None in May–June

**Seasonal Events (May 15–Jun 5):**
- 🌊 **Coral spawning** in May — underwater "snowstorm" of eggs and sperm
- Oceanic mantas still passing through Dampier Strait (tail end of Oct–Apr peak)
- Wobbegong sharks under ledges year-round
- Pygmy seahorses on gorgonian fans
- Schooling fish aggregations at pinnacle sites

**Notes:** Shoulder season = fewer crowds with minimal underwater compromise. Cape Kri holds world record at 374 fish species in a single dive.

---

### Banda Sea / Banda Islands
**Best Window:** Early May only — **MUST complete by May 31**
**Bad Window:** ⚠️ **AVOID JUNE ENTIRELY** (monsoon, rough seas, poor vis)

**Seasonal Events (May only):**
- Pelagics active: tuna, barracuda, reef sharks, eagle rays
- Hammerhead sharks present (peak Oct–Nov, but still around)
- Hawksbill & green turtles year-round
- Pristine soft corals & sea fans on volcanic walls

**Notes:** **Timing is everything.** May works, June does NOT. If Banda Sea is on the table, it must be early in the trip. Remote liveaboard access only.

---

### Alor
**Best Window:** Flexible (April–Oct dry season); June better than May for visibility
**Bad Window:** May has low vis (~15m from plankton blooms)

**Seasonal Events (May 15–Jun 5):**
- 🐟 **Mola mola** (ocean sunfish) at cleaning stations — cold upwellings active
- Sperm whale sightings & dolphin pods
- Hammerhead & thresher sharks at current-swept sites
- Eagle rays in channels
- Nutrient-rich plankton blooms support big species (reduces vis but attracts pelagics)

**Notes:** Visibility improves dramatically through the window (15m → 40m by mid-June). Later in the window = better. Very warm water (31–32°C).

---

### Halmahera
**Best Window:** **May 15–31 optimal** (end of Mar–May peak)
**Bad Window:** ⚠️ **Avoid June 1–15** (transitional monsoon); **June 16–Sep 15 = NO DIVING** (strong winds)

**Seasonal Events (May only):**
- 🦈 **Hammerhead sharks** active (March–November season)
- Manta rays & mobula rays at cleaning stations
- Pelagics active: tuna, barracuda schools, large reef sharks in channels
- **Halmahera epaulette shark** (unique species, common on night dives)
- Mandarinfish at dusk; macro life (frogfish, ghost pipefish, nudibranchs)

**Notes:** **Timing is critical** — May 15–31 is the sweet spot. After that, conditions degrade quickly. The "next Raja Ampat" with 450+ coral species and minimal crowds.

---

### Derawan Islands
**Best Window:** Flexible — May (transition), June–Aug (peak)
**Bad Window:** None in May–June

**Seasonal Events (May 15–Jun 5):**
- 🦈 **Whale sharks** arrive mid-May, present through mid-September
- **Manta rays** year-round at Sangalaki — feeding & cleaning stations active
- Turtle nesting on Sangalaki & Derawan beaches (green turtles)
- **Jellyfish lake at Kakaban** — non-stinging jellyfish (4 species), unique snorkel/dive

**Notes:** Minimal monsoon impact (near equator). Whale shark season starting mid-May is a bonus for this window. Unique jellyfish lake accessible year-round.

---

### Bangka Belitung (Belitung)
**Best Window:** **May 15–Jun 5 is PEAK/OPTIMAL** — best visibility, calm seas
**Bad Window:** July–Sep (strong currents, degraded visibility)

**Seasonal Events (May 15–Jun 5):**
- No major pelagic events — the draw is **unique underwater granite formations**
- Vibrant coral activity during dry season
- Blue-spotted stingrays active around granite boulders
- Reef fish populations at their most colorful

**Notes:** UNESCO Geopark. Massive granite pinnacles with coral carpeting create otherworldly underwater landscape. Crystal-clear water (up to 35m vis). Much more affordable than other destinations.
