# Wakatobi

## Overview
A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve with some of the highest consistent visibility in Indonesia (20–50m). Wakatobi is reef diving at its purest — 900+ fish species, 700+ coral species, minimal currents, and world-class macro. The name is an acronym of its four main islands: Wangi-Wangi, Kaledupa, Tomia, and Binongko. This is the antithesis of adrenaline diving — it's about slow, detailed reef exploration.

## Dates
- **Window:** Flexible — excellent conditions May through October
- **Season:** Peak — May is a transitional month offering some of the very best conditions of the year
- **Weather:** Driest season (May–Oct). Excellent surface conditions.

## Diving

### Conditions
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Visibility | 20–50m (averaging 35m / 100+ ft) — consistently outstanding |
| Water temp | 27–28°C (81–82°F) |
| Currents | Minimal — ideal for photography and leisurely exploration |
| Wetsuit | 3mm or even rashguard |

### Seasonal Events (May–June)
- Coral spawning events in May
- Year-round macro critter abundance (nudis, pygmy seahorses, ghost pipefish)
- No major pelagic events — this is a reef and macro destination
- Healthy resident population of turtles, reef sharks

### Key Dive Sites
| Site | Depth | Highlights | Difficulty |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| House Reef (Wakatobi Resort) | 3–20m+ wall | 400+ fish species, soft corals, gorgonians, tunicates | Easy |
| Tanjung Patok | 5–25m | Vivid soft corals, macro photography paradise, no current | Easy |
| Conchita | 5–30m | Three ridges, turtles, eagle rays, reef sharks in sandy bays | Moderate |
| Roma | 5–25m | Wall dive, barrel sponges, schooling fish | Easy–Moderate |
| Blade | 5–30m | Pristine hard coral slope, occasional pelagics | Moderate |

### Dive Plan
- 5–7 days, resort-based or liveaboard
- 2–3 dives/day + unlimited house reef diving
- Macro lens and wide-angle both rewarded here
- Nitrox available but not critical (moderate depths, no strong currents)

## Logistics

### Getting There
- Fly to **Bali (DPS)** → charter flight to Wakatobi (operated by the resort, ~2.5 hrs)
- Alternative: Bali → Makassar (UPG) → Kendari → boat to Wakatobi (longer, cheaper)
- The resort's charter flight is the standard way — runs on set days

### Getting Out
- Charter flight back to Bali on departure day
- Coordinate with resort for scheduling

### Accommodation
- **Wakatobi Dive Resort** — all-inclusive bungalows, from ~$300–500/night (includes diving, meals, house reef)
- Limited alternatives — this is a remote, single-resort destination

### Costs
| Item | Estimate (USD) |
|------|---------------|
| Wakatobi Resort (7 nights, all-inclusive) | $3,000–5,500 |
| Charter flight Bali ↔ Wakatobi | Often included in package; ~$500 if separate |
| Nitrox | Usually included |
| Marine park fee | Included in resort packages |

### Practical Info
- **Visa:** Indonesia e-VoA (same as other Indonesian legs)
- **Currency:** IDR. Resort handles everything — minimal need for cash.
- **Connectivity:** Resort WiFi available. No cell service.
- **Hyperbaric chamber:** The resort has oxygen equipment and emergency protocols. Nearest full chamber in Makassar (requires air evacuation).

## Notes
- Wakatobi is a different vibe — it's about reef quality, not adrenaline
- If you're into underwater photography, this is paradise (50m vis + no current = dream conditions)
- The house reef alone is worth the trip — unlimited shore diving included
- Book directly with the resort — they run a tight, all-inclusive operation
- Charter flight schedule may constrain dates — check departure days early
