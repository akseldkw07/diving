# Chuuk Lagoon

## Overview
The world's greatest wreck diving destination. Over 60 WWII Japanese warships, submarines, and aircraft sit on the lagoon floor — sunk during Operation Hailstone in February 1944. Warm, protected water and wrecks draped in soft coral make this a bucket-list destination for any diver. Located in the Federated States of Micronesia, not Indonesia.

## Dates
- **Window:** TBD — depends on how this fits with Komodo + Raja Ampat legs
- **Season:** Shoulder — transitioning toward wet season (Jun–Sep). May is better than June. Diving remains excellent year-round.
- **Weather:** Increasing humidity and rainfall. Brief tropical showers, rarely disrupting diving. Lagoon is protected.

## Diving

### Conditions
| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Visibility | 15–20m (reduced from dry season peak of 25–30m) |
| Water temp | 28–29°C (82–85°F) year-round, no thermoclines |
| Currents | Mild — lagoon is protected by barrier reef |
| Wetsuit | 3mm or even rashguard — very warm |
| Experience | Advanced OW minimum. Wreck penetration requires proper training. |

### Seasonal Events (May–June)
- No major seasonal marine events — the wrecks are the attraction year-round
- Soft coral growth on wrecks is lush
- Fewer divers than peak season (Oct–Apr) — more space on wrecks
- Reef sharks and barracuda patrol the wrecks

### Key Dive Sites (Wrecks)
| Site | Depth | Highlights | Difficulty |
|------|-------|------------|------------|
| Shinkoku Maru (fleet oiler, 152m) | 12–40m | Upright, accessible. Bridge, engine room, medical bay. Participated in Pearl Harbor. | Moderate |
| Fujikawa Maru (armed cargo ship) | 10–37m | Most photographed wreck. Aircraft and military equipment in holds. | Moderate |
| Heian Maru (sub tender, largest wreck) | 10–35m | Torpedoes and periscopes in cargo holds. Extensive penetration. | Moderate–Advanced |
| Nippo Maru (auxiliary tanker) | 25–50m | Anti-aircraft guns, howitzers, a Japanese tank visible on deck. | Advanced |
| Fumitsuki (destroyer, 97m) | 15–38m | One of two true warships sunk. Intact military structure. | Moderate–Advanced |
| Rio de Janeiro Maru (luxury liner) | 15–40m | Spacious holds, passenger liner features still visible. | Moderate–Advanced |
| San Francisco Maru (cargo ship) | 45–65m | Deep wreck — tanks, mines, ammunition. Technical diving only. | Technical |

### Reef Diving
- Barrier reef surrounding the lagoon offers coral diving
- Wrecks themselves are artificial reefs — covered in soft coral, sponges, anemones
- Marine life: barracuda, groupers, reef sharks, turtles on and around wrecks

## Logistics

### Getting There
- **No direct flights from Indonesia.** Multi-leg journey required.
- Typical route: Bali/Sorong → Manila or Guam → Weno, Chuuk
- Guam → Chuuk: United Airlines, ~1h 45m
- Alternative: via Papua New Guinea (~3 hr flight)
- **ESTA required** for transiting Guam (US territory) — apply at esta.cbp.dhs.gov
- Budget 1–2 travel days each way

### Getting Out
- Reverse the route: Chuuk → Guam → onward
- Or Chuuk → Papua New Guinea → Indonesia if continuing

### Accommodation
- **Liveaboard:** Primary option. Cabins included in package ($3,700–5,700 for 7 nights).
- **Land-based:** Blue Lagoon Resort, Truk Stop Hotel, Kurassa Hotel — basic.
- Land-based accommodation is limited and modest.

### Practical Info
- **Visa (FSM):** US citizens — no visa needed. EU citizens — visa on arrival (up to 30 days, extendable to 90).
- **Currency:** USD (official currency). **No ATMs on the islands.** Bring cash. Credit cards not reliably accepted outside liveaboards.
- **Connectivity:** Very limited. Satellite-based, slow. WiFi at Blue Lagoon Resort, Truk Stop, airport. No reliable cell service beyond Weno.
- **Hyperbaric chamber:** Local chamber on Chuuk exists but **unreliable** (sporadic staffing). Primary evacuation: **US Navy chamber in Guam** (~1 hr medevac flight). DAN insurance strongly recommended.
- **Power:** 110V US-style outlets.

## Notes
- This is a pilgrimage site for wreck divers — genuinely unlike anything else
- Wreck penetration: bring or rent a good primary light + backup
- Some wrecks are deep (San Francisco Maru at 45–65m) — only if tech trained
- The wrecks are war graves — treat them with respect, no artifact removal
- Logistics are the main challenge — plan the routing carefully
- Consider placing Chuuk before or after the Indonesia legs to minimize backtracking
- DAN membership is basically mandatory given the chamber situation
