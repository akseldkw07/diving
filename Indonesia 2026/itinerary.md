# Indonesia Diving Trip — Itinerary
**May 15 – June 5, 2026 (21 days)**

---

## Overview

Three weeks, three world-class regions: Nusa Penida for peak manta season, a Komodo liveaboard for drift diving and pelagics, and Raja Ampat for the planet's richest reef biodiversity. Each leg plays to the strengths of the May–June window.

| Leg | Dates | Location | Days | Style |
|-----|--------|----------|------|-------|
| 1 | May 15–20 | Nusa Penida, Bali | 5 | Land-based day boats |
| 2 | May 21–28 | Komodo National Park | 7 | Liveaboard |
| 3 | May 29–Jun 5 | Raja Ampat | 8 | Liveaboard or dive resort |

## Leg 1 — Nusa Penida (May 15–20)

### Why now?
May is **peak manta season** at Nusa Penida. Water temps sit around 26–29°C with 15–20m visibility. Mantas aggregate at cleaning stations almost daily. Dry season is underway, so surface conditions are calm.

### Key dive sites
- **Manta Point (south coast):** Cleaning station, almost guaranteed manta encounters at 5–15m depth. Reef sharks and blue-spotted stingrays below.
- **Crystal Bay:** Protected bay with beautiful corals. Mola mola season doesn't start until July, but the reef life is exceptional year-round.
- **SD Point / Gamat Bay:** Healthy hard coral gardens, schools of sweetlips and batfish.

### Suggested schedule
| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| May 15 (Fri) | Arrive Bali. Ferry or speedboat to Nusa Penida. Settle in. |
| May 16 (Sat) | 2–3 dives: Manta Point + Crystal Bay |
| May 17 (Sun) | 2–3 dives: Manta Point + SD Point |
| May 18 (Mon) | 2–3 dives: Gamat Bay + Crystal Bay |
| May 19 (Tue) | 2 dives morning. Afternoon free / snorkel. |
| May 20 (Wed) | Morning dive. Afternoon: ferry back to Bali, fly to Labuan Bajo (evening flight). |

### Operators to contact
- **Scuba Junkie Penida** — PADI 5★ IDC Resort, small groups, manta focus
- **Pure Dive Resort** — Max 4 divers per guide, free nitrox cert with 6+ dives
- **Sanctum Dive Nusa Penida** — Speedboats, 1:4 guide ratio

### Logistics
- **Getting there:** International flight → Bali (DPS). Fast boat from Sanur to Nusa Penida (~30 min).
- **Getting out:** Ferry back to Bali, then domestic flight Bali (DPS) → Labuan Bajo (LBJ). Multiple daily flights (Wings Air, AirAsia, Batik Air). ~1.5 hrs.
- **Accommodation:** Guesthouses and small resorts on Nusa Penida. Some operators offer packages with rooms.

## Leg 2 — Komodo Liveaboard (May 21–28)

### Why now?
May–June is **peak season** for Komodo. Dry season brings excellent visibility (15–30m), warm water (26–29°C), and manta rays are at their most active at cleaning stations. Strong currents make for thrilling drift diving.

### Key dive sites
- **Manta Point (Karang Makassar):** Massive 3km sandbank with manta cleaning stations. Shallow drift dives.
- **Batu Bolong:** Iconic pinnacle. Napoleon wrasse, whitetip reef sharks, huge schools of surgeonfish. Strong currents.
- **Castle Rock:** Submerged seamount. Grey reef sharks, giant trevally, massive schools of fusiliers. Advanced-level currents.
- **Crystal Rock:** Beautiful soft corals and reef fish, nearby Castle Rock.
- **The Cauldron / Shotgun:** Channel dive with acceleration through a narrow gap. Adrenaline dive.
- **Tatawa Besar:** Gentle drift along coral-covered slope. Good for macro and wide-angle.

### What a 7-night liveaboard typically covers
- **North route:** High visibility, big pelagic action, strong currents (Batu Bolong, Castle Rock, Crystal Rock)
- **South route:** Cooler, nutrient-rich water, massive manta aggregations at Manta Alley, macro life
- **Most 7-night trips cover both** north and south, with 3–4 dives per day

### Suggested liveaboard timing
| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| May 20 (Wed) | Arrive Labuan Bajo evening. Stay overnight near harbor. |
| May 21 (Thu) | Board liveaboard. First dives (south Komodo). |
| May 22–27 | Full diving days, 3–4 dives/day across north + south Komodo. |
| May 28 (Sat) | Final morning dive. Disembark Labuan Bajo by noon. |

### Liveaboard operators to contact
- **Wunderpus Liveaboard** — Traditional Phinisi boats, small intimate groups, Komodo + Alor routes
- **Dragon Dive Komodo (Shenron)** — PADI 5★ IDC center, 8-guest liveaboard, 7–10 night itineraries
- **Scuba Republic (Bajak)** — 28m vessel, 14 guests, SSI Diamond Center, customizable itineraries

### Logistics
- **Getting there:** Already in Labuan Bajo from Leg 1.
- **Getting out:** Fly Labuan Bajo (LBJ) → Sorong (SOQ). No direct flights — connect via Makassar (UPG) or Bali (DPS). Budget a full travel day.
- **Note:** May 28 is Eid al-Adha. Book flights early — domestic travel surges around this holiday.

## Leg 3 — Raja Ampat (May 29–Jun 5)

### Why now?
May–June is **shoulder season** in Raja Ampat — the wet-to-dry transition. Visibility is 15–25m, water is 27–28°C, and crowds are thinner than peak season (Oct–Apr). Coral spawning events happen in May, creating underwater spectacles. You won't get the peak manta aggregations at Dampier Strait (those are Oct–Apr), but reef biodiversity is unmatched year-round.

### Key dive sites
- **Cape Kri:** World record: 374 fish species in a single dive. Schooling jacks, barracuda, reef sharks over stunning coral.
- **Dampier Strait:** Epicenter of global marine biodiversity — 550 coral species, 1,427 fish species. Strong currents bring nutrients and pelagics.
- **Mioskon:** Wobbegong sharks resting under ledges, massive gorgonian fans, pygmy seahorses.
- **Sawandarek Jetty:** Shore dive directly off a village jetty. Schools of batfish, sweetlips, and rich macro life in the shallows.
- **Blue Magic:** Submerged seamount. Mantas, grey reef sharks, giant trevally in the blue.

### Suggested schedule
| Day | Plan |
|-----|------|
| May 28 (Sat) | Fly Labuan Bajo → Sorong (travel day, likely via Makassar). |
| May 29 (Sun) | Arrive Sorong. Transfer to resort/board liveaboard. Check dive. |
| May 30–Jun 4 | Full diving days, 3–4 dives/day across Dampier Strait, Wayag, Misool. |
| Jun 5 (Fri) | Final morning dive or snorkel. Transfer back to Sorong. Fly home (or onward). |

### Operators to contact
- **Raja Ampat Dive Lodge** — Mansuar Island, Dampier Strait location, house reef, beachfront rooms
- **Scuba Republic (Epica / Jaya)** — Budget-friendly liveaboard, 6–10 day Raja Ampat routes, SSI courses
- **Aggressor Fleet (Raja Ampat Aggressor)** — Premium luxury liveaboard, 7–10 night trips from Sorong

### Logistics
- **Getting there:** Fly from Labuan Bajo to Sorong. Connect via Makassar (UPG) or Bali (DPS). Arrive day before to buffer for flight delays.
- **Getting home:** Fly Sorong → Jakarta or Bali → international connection.
- **Raja Ampat marine park entry permit** (TAG): Purchase in Waisai. ~1,000,000 IDR for foreigners. Valid 1 year.
- **Important:** Arrive in Sorong a day early. Domestic flight delays/cancellations are common in eastern Indonesia.

## Seasonal Highlights for This Window

- 🐙 **Manta rays** — Peak season at both Nusa Penida and Komodo
- 🪸 **Coral spawning** — Raja Ampat, typically late May
- 🌊 **Dry season** — Starts across most of Indonesia, calm seas
- 🦈 **Reef sharks & pelagics** — Active at Komodo current-swept sites
- 🐢 **Turtle nesting** — Green and hawksbill turtles active across all three regions

## Things to Book ASAP
1. **Komodo liveaboard** — Popular boats fill months ahead for May/June
2. **Raja Ampat liveaboard or resort** — Limited capacity, especially around shoulder season
3. **Domestic flights on May 28** — Eid al-Adha creates a travel surge
4. **Nusa Penida operator** — Less urgent but good to lock in dates

## Packing Notes
- Certifications: Advanced Open Water + Rescue Diver cards
- DAN membership card
- 3mm wetsuit (sufficient for 26–29°C; maybe a hood for Raja Ampat thermoclines)
- SMB + reel (required by most Komodo operators for current diving)
- Dive computer, torch
- Reef-safe sunscreen
- Dramamine/Bonine if prone to seasickness (liveaboard nights)
